SCHOOL HISTORY


newsreport 1
Hyderabad: Playing a spirited game, Nathan Abraham (38 points) single-handedly steered Reqelford International School to a narrow 56-55 win over Delhi Public School, Hyderabad in a round robin match in the Basketball Federation of India’s Indian School Basketball League conducted by Telangana Basketball Association. With this win, Reqelford, led 31-26 at half-time, entered the knockout stage.

In a closely contested tie, the two teams vied to take control of the game. The lead changed hands several occasions. Using his agility and speed to good effect, Nathan scored through drive-ins and scored 10 of the 12 points by Reqelford in first quarter. However, Pranav and Rohan along with Arnab chipped in at regular for DPS, who led 14-12 at end of 1st quarter. In the second quarter Nathan continued his scoring spree and was just unstoppable. Reqelford wrested the lead at 31-26 at half time.


After change of ends DPS switched to “double teamed” Nathan which halted his scoring spree but it was here that Rohan and Choi took on the mantle to chip in with two baskets apiece to maintain the five-poin lead at end of the third quarter. In the fourth quarter too DPS held their grip and within fourminutes leveled the scores at 43-43.

With just 30 seconds left for the match Rohan of DPS scored through an offensive rebound to lead 55-54. However Nathan was fouled resulting in two free throws of which he converted one to level the scores 55-55. DPS made a last-ditch effort to score but of the rebound found its way to the front court and with just two seconds for the final whistle a foul on Nathan resulted in two free throws. Nathan converted the second free throw to ensure a one point win for Reqelford at 56-55.
newsreport 2

Hyderabad: State junior basketball player Nathan Abraham showed his shooting prowess with 37 points in the first match against Oakridge Einstein Campus as his side notched up impressive two victories on the opening day of the Basketball Federation of India’s Indian Basketball League conducted by Telangana Basketball Association at the Hyderabad Public School in Begumpet.
In the first match, they led 33-21 at the half time with the help of Abraham’s brilliant show as they finished the game 61-31. Later, they downed MGM High School 38-18 without much sweat.
